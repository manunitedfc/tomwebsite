Overview

The Netherlands has a rich history in soccer, with a national team that is renowned for its skillful play and innovative tactics. Known for their distinctive orange kits, the Dutch national team has been a prominent force in international soccer for decades.

Major Tournaments

FIFA World Cup

The Netherlands has reached the FIFA World Cup final three times (1974, 1978, and 2010) but has yet to win the trophy. The team is known for its attacking style of play, often referred to as "Total Football".

UEFA European Championship

The Dutch national team won the UEFA European Championship in 1988. This victory is remembered for the outstanding performance by Marco van Basten, who scored a memorable goal in the final.

Notable Players

Johan Cruyff

Johan Cruyff is widely regarded as one of the greatest soccer players of all time. His vision, technical skills, and leadership greatly influenced Dutch soccer and the development of "Total Football".

Marco van Basten

Marco van Basten was known for his incredible goal-scoring ability and is particularly remembered for his stunning volley in the 1988 European Championship final.

Ruud Gullit

Ruud Gullit was a versatile and dynamic player who played a key role in the 1988 European Championship win and was known for his powerful presence on the field.

Records

The Netherlands holds several notable records in soccer, including the highest number of goals scored in a single World Cup group stage (14 goals in 1974) and a remarkable unbeaten run in qualifying matches.
